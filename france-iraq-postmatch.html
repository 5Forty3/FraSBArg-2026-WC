🧭 The Story of the Match

France did exactly what champions-in-waiting do to overmatched opposition: took the lead early, controlled the game, and pulled clear without ever needing top gear — all while surviving the tournament's first major weather event, a thunderstorm that forced a delay of well over two hours and left the Philadelphia pitch saturated. Mbappé struck first with a trademark fizzing drive, doubled it after the restart courtesy of a gift from the Iraqi defence, and Ousmane Dembélé capped it with a long-awaited first World Cup goal. The xG told the story — 2.38 to 0.60, 20 touches in the Iraqi box to four — and, just as importantly, the back line finally kept the clean sheet that had eluded France for six straight matches. Qualification to the Round of 32 is sealed.

🌩️ The storm that split the game

This was a night defined as much by the weather as the football. A violent thunderstorm forced a delay of well over two hours, splitting the match either side of a saturated, water-splashing pitch and removing the second-half hydration break entirely. To France's credit, the long interruption didn't break their rhythm — they came out after the restart and were "almost immediately back on the front foot," getting their gift of a second goal within minutes. Maintaining concentration through a two-hour stoppage is its own test, and the champions passed it without fuss.

⚽ Goal 1 (14') — Mbappé's fizzing drive

France · 1-0Olise finds him simply, Mbappé rifles it from distance
Olise's pass inside Mbappé's drive from distance

The breakthrough, and the Olise–Mbappé axis at work. It was almost too simple: Michael Olise, drifting in from the right, slid an unfussy ball inside to Mbappé, who took a touch and rifled a fizzing left-footed drive from outside the box past Ahmed Basil in the 14th minute. Exactly the pre-match plan — Olise as the central conductor feeding the finisher — executed early to settle any nerves. It was Mbappé's 15th World Cup goal and the perfect demonstration that France don't need to break a deep block down patiently when they have a player who can simply detonate one from 20 yards. One moment of quality, and the contest was effectively decided.

⚽ Goal 2 (2nd half) — A gift, gratefully accepted

France · 2-0Iraq's botched goal-kick, France pounce, Dembélé squares for Mbappé
Iraq's botched goal-kick Dembélé recovery & square Mbappé finish

Punishing the error — the chronic Iraqi flaw struck again. Just as against Norway, Iraq were undone by their own building from the back. Shortly after the storm restart, a botched short goal-kick was fired straight to the feet of a defender, France pressed high and won it back, and Dembélé — unselfish as ever — squared for Mbappé to slot home his 16th World Cup goal, equalling Klose. The pre-match warning was that Iraq's defensive errors, not France's brilliance, would likely decide the margin; so it proved. It also showcased the value of France's aggressive high press after the restart: keep the pressure on a nervous defence, and the gifts arrive. Two-nil, and the game was dead.

⚽ Goal 3 — Dembélé's long-awaited first World Cup goal

France · 3-0Olise slides him through, Dembélé tucks it into the bottom corner
Olise's through-ball Dembélé run & finish

A milestone moment, and Olise's masterclass continues. Having squared selflessly for Mbappé's second, Dembélé got his own reward: Michael Olise slid him through on the right with an effortless pass, and the PSG winger tucked it powerfully across Basil into the bottom-left corner — his first ever World Cup goal, at the 19th time of asking across major tournaments. The assist was Olise's third of the tournament, underlining that the decision to make him the central creative hub has been transformative. Beyond the milestone, the goal reflected France's total control: even at 2-0 with the game won, they kept probing, and the quality of the Olise-to-Dembélé connection was simply a class above.

🔧 Future Tactic 1 — The blueprint holds: keep Olise central, Mbappé free

France · Future 1The structure that's clicked — preserve it for Norway & beyond
Olise distribution Mbappé's run in behind

The shape has clicked — don't touch it. Across the Senegal second half and now this full game, the pattern is settled and devastating: Olise as the central playmaker, Dembélé and Barcola stretching the flanks, and Mbappé as the highest, freest runner. Olise's vision turns France's individual talent into a coherent attack — he created the opener and the third, and pulls all the strings. For Norway on Friday (the likely top-spot decider) and into the knockouts, Deschamps should keep this exact structure; the first-half congestion of the Senegal opener feels solved. The one nuance for tougher opponents: against a side that defends deeper and more disciplined than Iraq, France will need Olise's between-the-lines passing and the overlapping full-backs even more, since they won't get gift goals from defensive errors.

🛡️ Future Tactic 2 — The defence: clean sheet earned, but the real test is Norway

France · Future 2Tighten the rest-defence before Haaland comes calling
Screening / cover Ball over the top denied

A clean sheet — but read it honestly. France finally ended their six-game drought without conceding, which is a genuine psychological boost. But it came against a limited Iraq side that mustered just 0.6 xG and four touches in the French box; this tells us little about the back line's resilience against elite opposition. The far sterner exam arrives Friday: Norway and Erling Haaland, likely for top spot. The fixes that matter against a world-class No.9 who lives on the shoulder: a disciplined screening pivot (Koné/Tchouaméni) protecting the space in front of the centre-backs, and the full-backs holding shape rather than over-committing — the exact looseness Senegal exploited. France have the personnel to be the tournament's best defence; the clean sheet is a start, but Haaland will be the true measure. Get the rest-defence tight, and a France side scoring this freely becomes a genuine favourite to reach a third straight final.

🗺️ What Happens Next

Qualification sealed, Mbappé level with Klose and chasing Messi, Dembélé off the mark, and a first clean sheet banked — a near-perfect night's work, storm and all. The picture is bright: France look settled in their attacking shape, ruthless in front of goal, and now have the defensive clean sheet to build belief on. The final group game against Norway is the real prize — likely a straight shootout for top spot and the more favourable knockout path, and a heavyweight test of France's defence against Haaland. Win it, and France enter the knockouts as one of the tournament's most feared sides, with the in-form Mbappé-Olise-Dembélé axis humming.

📊 Bottom Line

A professional, comfortable, record-extending night for France — exactly what was needed. Mbappé's brace moved him level with Klose and kept him on Messi's trail, Dembélé finally broke his World Cup duck, Olise pulled every string, and the long-awaited clean sheet arrived — all while navigating a two-hour storm delay without losing focus. The honest caveats are that Iraq were limited and gifted France a goal, so the defensive solidity remains unproven against the elite. But the attacking blueprint is humming and qualification is secured. The Norway decider will tell us how good this France side really is; on this evidence, they look every inch contenders. Mbappé, 16 World Cup goals — and the chase is on.

📋 Team News & Probable XIs

This is the standout fixture of Matchday 1 — five-time champions against the team that knocked on the final's door in 2022 and have since become the "Brazilians of Africa." Morocco arrive as reigning AFCON champions, unbeaten in their last five, sitting 8th in the world. Brazil are favourites, but everyone covering this game agrees: this is a genuine test, not a warm-up.

🇧🇷 Brazil — the picture

  • Neymar is OUT — grade-two calf strain; he wears the No.10 but isn't fit. Brazil build without a classic playmaker, so creativity runs through Paquetá and the wide forwards.
  • Right-back is the headache. Wesley withdrew injured. Ancelotti has tested Danilo, Ederson and Roger Ibañez there — Danilo is the likeliest starter, but it's a patched-up spot.
  • The real weakness: left-back. An ageing Alex Sandro (or Douglas Santos) has to deal with Hakimi — a recurring theme in every preview.
  • Spine is elite: Alisson; Marquinhos & Gabriel; Casemiro + Bruno Guimarães; Vinícius & Raphinha wide; Matheus Cunha (or Igor Thiago) at striker.

Probable XI (4-2-3-1): Alisson; Danilo, Marquinhos, Gabriel, Alex Sandro; Casemiro, Bruno Guimarães; Raphinha, Paquetá, Vinícius Jr; Cunha.

🇲🇦 Morocco — the picture

  • Two big absences: CB Nayef Aguerd (groin) and winger Abde Ezzalzouli (knee) are out — Aguerd's loss weakens the centre, but Ouahbi insists the principles don't change.
  • Mazraoui passed fit after a shoulder knock vs Norway — expected to start at left-back.
  • The Hakimi question: normally right-back, but some line-ups have him switched to track Vinícius directly. Either way he's the fulcrum — defence and their No.1 attacking outlet.
  • Defend-as-a-unit identity: compact, disciplined, lethal on the counter through Hakimi's overlaps and Brahim Díaz between the lines. Young double pivot (Bouaddi / El Aynaoui) is the area to test.

Probable XI (4-3-3): Bounou; Hakimi, Diop, Riad, Mazraoui; El Aynaoui, Bouaddi, Ounahi; Brahim Díaz, Saibari, El Khannouss.

♟️ The Central Chess Problem

Strip it down and the game is a single recurring question: can Brazil's individual quality unlock a Morocco block that is purpose-built to deny exactly this kind of opponent? Morocco won't try to dominate the ball — their own coach says their route "is not long possession." They want a compact 4-3-3 / 4-5-1 mid-block, deny the half-spaces, and turn one Brazilian loose pass into a Hakimi-led counter.

Brazil's answer is positional: control the middle through Casemiro + Bruno Guimarães, then isolate Vinícius 1v1 on the left and let Raphinha drift into vertical pockets on the right. The fault-line runs down both flanks — Vinícius attacking Hakimi's side, and Hakimi attacking Brazil's weak left-back. Whoever wins their wide duel tilts the group.

⚔️ The duel that decides it — Vinícius Jr vs Achraf Hakimi

The whole match compresses into this. Vinícius is football's most dangerous 1v1 runner in transition; Hakimi is one of the few full-backs with the recovery pace and intelligence to live with him — and Morocco's primary attacking weapon. The tension: if Hakimi pushes high to attack, he leaves the exact space Vinícius wants to run into. If he stays to contain Vinícius, Morocco lose their best outlet. Brazil will deliberately attack this seam again and again.

🎯 Tactical Move 1 — How Brazil Attacks

Brazil · AttackThe left-side overload to free Vinícius
Ball-carry / dribble Pass / cross Off-ball run (decoy / underlap)

The idea: Brazil deliberately overloads Vinícius's flank. Paquetá drifts left and Alex Sandro underlaps on the outside — two runners that occupy El Aynaoui and pin Hakimi, forcing Morocco to choose who to track. That isolates Vinícius 1v1 against Hakimi with a runner outside him. If Hakimi steps to the overlap, Vinícius drives inside the channel vacated behind him; if Hakimi holds, Vinícius takes him on. Either way Brazil manufactures a yard, then the cut-back / far-post ball (yellow dashed) attacks the space Aguerd's absence has weakened in the centre — Riad and Diop dragged across, Cunha and Raphinha arriving at the back post. This is the single move Brazil will run most often.

🛡️ Tactical Move 2 — How Morocco Defends (& Strikes Back)

Morocco · Defend + CounterCompact block, then the Hakimi–Brahim transition
Ball won → first pass Counter run / carry Hakimi overlap / cut-back Block shape

The idea: Morocco sit in two compact banks, conceding the ball and protecting the half-spaces — Bouaddi and El Aynaoui screen the zone where Bruno and Paquetá want to receive. The trap: invite Brazil's full-backs (especially Alex Sandro) high, then win the ball and go vertical in three touches. The instant Bouaddi intercepts, the first pass finds Brahim Díaz between the lines (green) — he turns and releases Hakimi storming into the space behind the advanced Alex Sandro (red). With Vinícius caught upfield and slow to track back — a weakness every preview names — that flank is open grass. The cut-back targets the box before Brazil's centre-backs reset. This is precisely how Morocco hurt elite sides: not possession, but lethal, rehearsed transition.

🔑 The Sub-Battles That Will Swing It

  • Bruno Guimarães vs Morocco's young pivot (Bouaddi): Bruno is Brazil's engine — he finds the pockets Casemiro can't reach. Bouaddi is technically gifted but raw; this is his examination. If Bruno gets time between the lines, Brazil flow.
  • Brazil's rest-defence vs the counter: Casemiro screening, and whether the makeshift right-back (Danilo) and Alex Sandro can stay disciplined. One loose pass becomes a Hakimi break.
  • Brahim Díaz on the half-turn: Morocco's release valve. Marquinhos/Casemiro must deny him time, or the counters ignite.
  • Set-pieces: Hakimi, El Khannouss and Ounahi are dead-ball threats — with Aguerd's aerial presence gone, but Brazil's centre-backs strong, this is a quieter-but-live route for Morocco.

📊 Verdict & Predicted Scoreline

The consensus from previews and models: Brazil have too much quality in midfield and on the wings over 90 minutes, but Morocco are exactly the organised, transition-sharp opponent who can make the favourites uncomfortable — several models lean "Morocco or draw." Expect Brazil to dominate the ball and territory, Morocco to defend in banks and threaten on the Hakimi break. The decisive variable is whether Vinícius wins his duel before Hakimi wins his.

🇧🇷 Brazil 2 — 1 Morocco

Most likely: Brazil edge it through a Vinícius moment + a second-half cushion. A 1-1 draw is very much live if Morocco's first counter lands. The "cagey, high-quality" game everyone expects.

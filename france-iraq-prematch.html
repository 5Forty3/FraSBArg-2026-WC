France vs Iraq

The champions chase qualification & Mbappé chases history — against the Lions of Mesopotamia's low block

🎯 The stakes — one win from the last 32

France beat Senegal 3-1 in a "group of death" opener and a win here seals Round-of-32 qualification with a game to spare. They currently sit second behind Norway only on goal difference, so a big scoreline matters with the likely Matchday-3 decider against Haaland's Norway looming. Iraq, back at a World Cup for the first time in 40 years, were beaten 4-1 by Norway and need a result to keep faint qualification hopes alive — but realistically face one of the tournament's tallest tasks.

📋 Team News verify before kickoff

These XIs are predicted, not yet confirmed — though there's strong agreement across outlets. The key theme: with qualification in sight against the group's weakest side, Deschamps is expected to make around three changes to rest legs for the knockouts — while crucially keeping the attacking combination that tore Senegal apart after half-time.

🇫🇷 France — the expected rotation

  • Olise central, Dembélé wide, Barcola starting — the exact trio (behind Mbappé) that dismantled Senegal in the second half. Olise was Man of the Match in the opener and is in scintillating form; Barcola earned a start after his lively cameo and debut goal.
  • ~3 changes to rest legs: Lucas Digne in for Théo Hernández at left-back; Manu Koné likely for Tchouaméni (a World Cup debut) alongside Rabiot; Barcola in for Doué. All sources agree on the same three.
  • Saliba "gritting his teeth" through long-standing niggles but expected to start; the Saliba-Upamecano pairing holds.
  • Mbappé leads the line and chases history (see below). France have scored in 14 straight matches, multiple goals in 13 of them.
  • The honest caveat: France have not kept a clean sheet in six games — Senegal exposed real defensive chinks. Even rotating, the back line must concentrate.

Expected XI (4-2-3-1): Maignan; Koundé, Saliba, Upamecano, Digne; Koné, Rabiot; Dembélé, Olise, Barcola; Mbappé. (Not yet confirmed.)

🇮🇶 Iraq — don't dismiss them entirely

  • A compact, deep 4-4-2 under Graham Arnold (the ex-Australia boss): stay narrow, protect the box, frustrate, and spring counters. The plan is "survival and opportunism."
  • Aymen Hussein is the focal point — a physical target man (33 international goals) who scored Iraq's first World Cup goal in 40 years against Norway with a bullet header. His hold-up play and aerial threat are Iraq's route back into any game.
  • Amir Al-Ammari is the key creator and outlet; Man Utd's Zidane Iqbal brings European pedigree in midfield. Ali Al-Hamadi (their other striker) is a fitness doubt.
  • A note of defiance: Arnold has said Iraq "refuse to stay conservative" and will "engage" France rather than merely park the bus — which could make them braver (and more vulnerable) than a pure low block.
  • The warning sign: vs Norway they managed no shots after the 63rd minute as legs and structure faded. Discipline for 90 minutes is the challenge.

Expected XI (4-4-2): Hassan; Hussein Ali, Tahseen, Hashim, Doski; Bayesh, Al-Ammari, Iqbal, Qasem; Al-Hamadi, A. Hussein. (Not yet confirmed.)

👑 Mbappé's date with history

Against Senegal, Mbappé became France's all-time leading scorer (58 international goals), surpassing Giroud, and broke Just Fontaine's France World Cup record. Now he sits on 14 World Cup goals — just two behind the all-time leaders, Messi and Klose (16). With Messi having moved to 18 in Argentina's win earlier today, the Mbappé-Messi duel for "greatest World Cup scorer of this era" is the tournament's great running subplot — and Iraq's porous defence (4 conceded vs Norway) is exactly the kind of opponent against which Mbappé feasts. He's scored in seven consecutive France games.

♟️ The Central Problem — Breaking the Block, Punishing the Counter

This is a classic favourite-vs-underdog tactical puzzle, with one wrinkle: Iraq say they'll have a go rather than purely sit. France will dominate the ball and must break a packed, narrow defence; the question is how quickly. The flip side is France's own soft underbelly — six games without a clean sheet — and Iraq's one genuine weapon, Hussein on the counter and from set-pieces. Let's draw the key patterns.

⚡ Tactical Move 1 — How France breaks the block: Mbappé in behind

France · AttackStretch it wide, then the vertical ball for Mbappé's run
Mbappé run / finish Olise's vertical ball Width pins the full-backs

France's bluntest instrument against a low block. The lesson from the first-half struggle vs Senegal was clear — France got congested centrally. The fix, which worked after the break and should start here: Dembélé and Barcola hold genuine width to pin Iraq's full-backs and stretch the narrow block, while Olise pulls the strings from a central role, sliding vertical balls into the channels. The instant Iraq's back line drops too deep or steps fractionally square, Mbappé spins off the shoulder and attacks the space — the single hardest thing in football to defend. Iraq's centre-backs showed clear vulnerability to direct, pacey play against Norway; Mbappé is a far more refined version of the same threat. Get him isolated against that back line once, and the breakthrough comes.

🎨 Tactical Move 2 — The Olise–Mbappé axis & overlapping width

France · CreationOlise the conductor, full-backs overlapping to overload
Olise distribution / Mbappé finish Cut-back Koundé overlap

The creative engine that unlocked Senegal — bedded in from the start. Michael Olise was the standout of France's opener, and shifting him permanently into the central playmaker role is the key tweak. From there he receives between Iraq's lines, scans, and feeds the wide men or threads the killer pass. The pattern to watch: Olise slides it wide to Dembélé, Koundé overlaps outside to pin the full-back and reach the byline, and the cut-back finds Mbappé attacking the near post with a late runner (Rabiot or Barcola) behind. Against a narrow block that protects the centre, overlapping width and cut-backs are the proven way in — and France, with Olise conducting, have more ways to hurt a deep defence than almost anyone: pace in behind, dribblers wide, runners from midfield. Patience is rarely a problem for long.

🦁 Tactical Move 3 — Iraq's one route: Hussein on the counter & set-pieces

Iraq · CounterHold it up with Hussein, hit France's open back line
Win it → feed Hussein Runner attacks the channel

Iraq's slim but real path — and France's genuine vulnerability. Les Bleus have not kept a clean sheet in six matches, and Senegal showed their back line can be got at when the full-backs push high and the structure stretches. Iraq's plan leans entirely on that: win the ball, find Aymen Hussein's hold-up play (his bullet header against Norway proved he can punish this level), and spring a runner — Al-Hamadi or a breaking midfielder — into the channel behind France's advancing full-backs. Set-pieces are their other lifeline; Hussein's aerial power from an Al-Ammari delivery is a constant threat. The catch: Iraq managed no shots after the 63rd minute against Norway as fatigue bit. They must stay disciplined and clinical in their rare moments — a tall order, but with Arnold vowing to "engage" rather than merely defend, they'll at least try to make France uncomfortable.

⚔️ Tactical Move 4 — The duel that defines it: Mbappé vs Iraq's centre-backs

Key DuelMbappé's movement vs Tahseen & Hashim
Mbappé run Through-ball

The contest that decides how comfortable the night is. Iraq's centre-back pairing (likely Zaid Tahseen and Akam Hashim) showed clear vulnerabilities against Norway's direct play. Against Mbappé they face an even harder examination: he'll drift to the left channel to escape a marker, then dart on the blindside across the front of the centre-back into the space behind. If Iraq's full-backs are dragged out by Dembélé and Barcola, the centre-backs are isolated, and tracking Mbappé's movement while holding the line is close to impossible for defenders of this level. France only need to win this duel once for the floodgates to open — and given Mbappé's record-chasing motivation against a porous defence, expect him to test it relentlessly from the first whistle.

🔑 Key Players & Sub-Battles

  • Kylian Mbappé — two from the all-time World Cup record, in ruthless form, against exactly the kind of defence he punishes. The headline act.
  • Michael Olise — France's in-form conductor; if he starts central, he'll be the creative hub that unlocks the block.
  • Aymen Hussein — Iraq's talisman and only real route to a goal; his hold-up and aerial threat are the counter and set-piece danger.
  • Manu Koné — likely a France World Cup debut; his energy alongside Rabiot must also screen the counters Iraq will seek.
  • France's back line vs complacency — six games without a clean sheet. A rotated defence easing off could gift Iraq the consolation they crave.

📊 Verdict & Predicted Scoreline

The gulf is enormous and France should win comfortably — they have too much pace, too much craft, and too many ways to break a deep block, with Mbappé and Olise leading a forward line Iraq simply cannot match. The only real questions are the margin and whether France's rotated, clean-sheet-shy defence keeps Iraq scoreless. Expect Les Bleus to be patient if needed, find the breakthrough, and pull clear once the first goal lands — with Mbappé the overwhelming favourite to author it and edge closer to history. Iraq will "engage," may frustrate for a spell, and could nick a consolation on the counter or a set-piece, but a result is a near-impossible ask.

🇫🇷 France 4 — 0 Iraq

Most likely: a controlled, possibly patient first 25 minutes, then France's quality — and a likely Mbappé goal or two — runs clear. A Both-Teams-To-Score scenario (a late Iraq counter past a rotated French defence) is very live given the six-game clean-sheet drought.

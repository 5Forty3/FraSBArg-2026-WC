📊 Group H after MD1 — all four level on 1 point

Spain's stunning 0-0 with Cape Verde left them bottom of a four-way tie (Saudi Arabia, Uruguay, Cape Verde and Spain all on a single point). With Bielsa's Uruguay still to come on the final day, this is now a must-win: four points from the next two games would likely secure qualification. The pressure on the pre-tournament favourites is real — and after watching a 40-year-old keeper deny them, Saudi's Al-Owais "will certainly be channeling his inner Vozinha."

📋 Team News — The Big Change Spain Needed

Our Cape Verde post-match verdict was blunt: start the game-breakers, field a real box presence, play through the block not around it. De la Fuente has listened on the most important point — both Lamine Yamal and Nico Williams are set to START, the exact change the performance demanded. The two wide threats who turned the opener the moment they came on now begin together for the first time at this World Cup.

🇪🇸 Spain — the changes

  • Yamal STARTS — confirmed ready from the first whistle, though "still not able to complete a full 90," so expect him managed off around the hour. In just 20 minutes off the bench vs Cape Verde he had 5 dribbles and 2 shots — Spain's only spark.
  • Nico Williams STARTS too — pace and directness on the left, attacking the opposite flank to Yamal so Saudi can't double up on one side.
  • Ferran Torres & Gavi drop out — neither "covered themselves in glory" vs Cape Verde. Oyarzabal continues at striker (the man who memorably went 30 minutes without a touch in the opener, but carries real goal threat — 6 goals in 5 of his last 6 qualifiers).
  • Midfield unchanged & elite: Rodri, Pedri, Fabián Ruiz — the engine that must finally turn possession into penetration. Merino is nursing a knock; Víctor Muñoz is out (hamstring).

Probable XI (4-3-3): Simón; Llorente, Cubarsí, Laporte, Cucurella; Fabián Ruiz, Rodri, Pedri; Yamal, Oyarzabal, N. Williams.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — the deep block, again

  • A battling 1-1 draw with Uruguay (they led until late) — satisfied with their tournament so far, and likely unchanged.
  • The plan is pure containment: just 33% possession and 3 shots on target vs Uruguay; they generated a microscopic 0.06 xG in the second half. Shrink the pitch, deny Spain access, survive.
  • Salem Al-Dawsari is their one real counter-attacking danger — Spain must respect transitions if they over-commit, but Saudi rarely threaten sustained pressure.
  • Sobering record: Saudi Arabia have lost 9 straight World Cup matches to UEFA sides, 8 of them to nil. The blueprint is to make it ugly and steal a point.

Probable XI (4-4-2): Al-Owais; Abdulhamid, Al-Amri, Al-Tambakti, Al-Harbi; Abu Al-Shamat, Kanno, Al-Khaibari, S. Al-Dawsari; Al-Brikan, Al-Juwayr.

Spain didn't lack chances against Cape Verde; they lacked cutting edge. The numbers are a portrait of dominance without a finish — and they are the benchmark every improvement today is measured against.

27 shots and 2.29 xG without scoring — and that was without Yamal and Williams for 70 minutes. The dam was always likely to burst once the dribblers started. The three fixes the opener demanded: (1) start the 1v1 game-breakers, (2) fill the penalty box on cut-backs, (3) add verticality so the build-up isn't a predictable sideways "U".

⚡ Improvement 1 — Start Yamal & Nico: attack both touchlines at once

Spain · Fix 1Two 1v1 threats stretch a block that can't double both
Winger 1v1 drive Switch of play

The single most important change. As RotoWire put it: "Spain's most direct route to a goal is Yamal isolating his full-back... Saudi have to double up on him without leaving Nico Williams one-on-one on the other side — and that is a difficult balance to strike for ninety minutes." That is the entire point of starting both. Yamal is a left-footed inverted right winger who manufactures space 1v1 against any block; Nico Williams is raw pace and directness on the left. With both on the pitch, Saudi cannot collapse onto one flank — double Yamal and a quick switch frees Nico in space; respect Nico and Yamal cuts inside to shoot or thread the killer ball. Against Cape Verde, Spain attacked with no touchline 1v1 threat for 70 minutes; today they have two of the world's best from the first whistle.

🎯 Improvement 2 — Fill the box & play vertical (don't just pass it sideways)

Spain · Fix 2Bodies on the cut-back + Pedri's vertical penetration
Yamal carry / cut-back Box runners (9 + back post) Pedri late at the edge

The other half of the fix. Even with two great wingers, Spain must put bodies in the box — the very thing missing vs Cape Verde, where cut-backs found nobody. The pattern: when Yamal or Nico reaches the byline, Oyarzabal attacks the near post, the opposite winger arrives at the back post, and Pedri times a late run to the edge of the area for the cut-back. And to stop the build-up becoming the predictable sideways "U" of the opener, Pedri must be braver vertically — receiving on the half-turn in the pockets and sliding the line-breaking pass, while the threat of a shot from range forces Saudi to step out of their shell. Volume was never Spain's problem (27 shots); quality of the final action was. Fill the box, play forward, be clinical — and the 0-0 ghost is exorcised quickly.

🔑 Key Watch-Points

  • The clock on Yamal — he can't yet do a full 90, so the first hour is when Spain must make it count before he's managed off.
  • Oyarzabal's finishing — quiet vs Cape Verde, but a proven scorer. If Spain create early and he converts, nerves vanish.
  • Transition discipline — Spain will throw caution forward; Al-Dawsari is Saudi's one outlet. Don't gift the counter that gives Saudi a famous point.
  • Tempo & patience — if the breakthrough is slow again, the Cape Verde memory could tighten Spanish legs. An early goal is everything.

📊 Verdict & Predicted Scoreline

Spain generated 27 shots and 2.29 xG without their two best attackers for most of the opener — now both start, against a Saudi side that has lost nine straight World Cup games to European opposition, eight to nil, and managed 0.06 xG in a half against Uruguay. The blueprint that frustrated Spain (a packed block plus an inspired keeper) is repeatable, but with Yamal and Williams from the first whistle and bodies in the box, the dam should burst this time. Expect Spain to be far more clinical and pull clear once the first goal arrives.

🇪🇸 Spain 3 — 0 Saudi Arabia

Most likely: a tense opening, then Yamal/Williams unlock it and Spain run out comfortable winners. A nervy 1-0 or another frustrating draw is the downside if the finishing misfires again — but the personnel finally fit the problem.
